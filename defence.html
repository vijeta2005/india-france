The Rafale story

No single deal defines the India-France defence relationship more than the purchase of Rafale fighter jets — one of the biggest defence deals in Indian history.

Headline deal

✈️ The Rafale Fighter Jet Deal

In 2016, India signed a deal with France's Dassault Aviation to purchase 36 Rafale multirole fighter jets. The first jets arrived in India in 2020 and were inducted into the Indian Air Force at Ambala Air Base. The Rafale is considered one of the most advanced fighter aircraft in the world — capable of air superiority, ground attack, nuclear deterrence, and reconnaissance missions.

Defence partnership timeline

The defence relationship between India and France has been built steadily over decades.

1953

First defence cooperation

India purchases its first Ouragan jet fighters from France — marking the beginning of a long defence partnership that continues to this day.

1998

Strategic Partnership signed

India and France sign a comprehensive Strategic Partnership covering defence, space, nuclear energy, and trade — elevating the relationship to a new level.

2005

VARUNA naval exercises begin

India and France launch joint naval exercises called VARUNA in the Indian Ocean, strengthening maritime security cooperation between the two navies.

2008

Scorpene submarine deal

India signs a deal with France's Naval Group to build six Scorpene-class submarines in India under technology transfer — a major boost to India's naval capabilities.

2016

Rafale deal signed

PM Modi and French President Hollande finalise the historic deal for 36 Rafale fighter jets — the largest single defence procurement in Indian Air Force history.

2023

26 more Rafales ordered

India signs a fresh deal for 26 Rafale Marine jets for the Indian Navy — to be deployed on INS Vikrant, India's first domestically built aircraft carrier.

Joint military exercises

India and France regularly conduct joint military exercises across all three armed forces.

Navy

VARUNA

Annual bilateral naval exercise between the Indian Navy and French Navy in the Indian Ocean. Covers anti-submarine warfare, air defence, and maritime patrol.

Army

SHAKTI

Joint army exercise between Indian Army and French Army. Focuses on counter-terrorism operations and jungle warfare in varied terrains.

Air Force

GARUDA

Bilateral air exercise between the Indian Air Force and French Air and Space Force. Involves advanced fighter jet operations including the Rafale.

Did you know?

France is one of the very few countries that has consistently supported India's bid for a permanent seat on the UN Security Council. This political backing — combined with deep defence ties — makes France one of India's most strategically important partners in the world.

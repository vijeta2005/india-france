🎨 India & France — Culture

From French cafés in Tamil Nadu to Indian yoga studios in Paris — two of the world's richest cultures have been quietly influencing each other for centuries.

Where cultures meet

The cultural exchange between India and France runs deep — in food, language, cinema, philosophy, and everyday life.

🍽️

Food & cuisine

French cuisine found its way into Pondicherry through colonial rule — baguettes, crepes, and café culture became part of daily life. In reverse, Indian spices and curry have been wildly popular in France for decades. Paris now has hundreds of Indian restaurants, and dishes like chicken tikka masala are eaten across the country.

🎬

Cinema & film

Indian cinema — especially Tamil and Hindi films — has a dedicated fan base in France. The Cannes Film Festival has featured Indian films for decades, with directors like Satyajit Ray and Mira Nair earning international acclaim. French New Wave cinema has in turn influenced generations of Indian filmmakers.

Cinema
🧘

Yoga & philosophy

Indian philosophy, yoga, and spirituality have had a massive influence on French culture. France has one of the highest numbers of yoga practitioners in Europe. Figures like Sri Aurobindo — who was educated in France — built philosophical bridges between the two civilisations. The French town of Auroville in Pondicherry was founded as an international community inspired by his teachings.

Philosophy
🎵

Music & arts

Indian classical music has fascinated French audiences since the 20th century. Ravi Shankar famously collaborated with French musicians and performed at major French venues. Today, Indian fusion music is a growing genre in France, while French electronic music influences Indian DJs and producers.

Music
👗

Fashion

French fashion houses like Hermès, Dior, and Chanel have long drawn inspiration from Indian textiles, embroidery, and craftsmanship. Indian artisans supply hand-embroidered fabrics to top Parisian fashion houses. In return, French fashion trends are hugely popular among Indian designers and urban youth.

City spotlight

🌴 Pondicherry — where India meets France

Pondicherry (now Puducherry) is unlike any other city in India. The "White Town" area still has French colonial architecture, French street names, French-style pavements, and even French-speaking residents whose families never left after 1954.

Tamil and French are both official languages here. You can walk from a traditional Tamil temple to a French bakery in under five minutes. It's the most visible symbol of the India-France cultural connection.

Alliance Française — French culture across India

Alliance Française is a network of French cultural centres spread across India. They teach the French language, screen French films, host art exhibitions, and celebrate French festivals like Bastille Day. With centres in over 15 Indian cities, they are the most active cultural organisation connecting the two countries at a grassroots level.

Did you know?

The famous French fashion house Hermès has a special line called "Shawls of India" — hand-crafted by Indian artisans using traditional techniques like Kashmiri embroidery. Some of these shawls take over 18 months to make and sell for thousands of euros in Paris boutiques.

The Charpak scholarship

The Charpak scholarship is the French government's flagship scholarship programme for Indian students — fully funded and highly prestigious.

French Government Scholarship

🏆 Charpak Scholarship Programme

Named after Nobel Prize-winning physicist Georges Charpak, this scholarship is awarded by the French Embassy in India every year. It covers tuition fees, living expenses, travel, and health insurance for Indian students going to study in France. There are three types — for exchange students, for Masters students, and for research internships.

Applications open every year around January–March. You apply through the Campus France India website. The scholarship is extremely competitive but highly rewarding — giving Indian students access to some of the world's best universities completely free.

Academic partnerships

The education relationship between India and France goes far beyond scholarships — covering university tie-ups, language learning, and joint research.

🏫

IIT & grandes écoles partnerships

Several IITs have formal exchange and joint degree programmes with France's top engineering schools — the grandes écoles. IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, and IIT Madras all have active partnerships with institutions like École Polytechnique, CentraleSupélec, and INSA. Students can spend a semester in France and receive credit at their home IIT.

Engineering
🔬

Joint research programmes

The CEFIPRA (Centre Franco-Indien pour la Promotion de la Recherche Avancée) funds joint research projects between Indian and French scientists. Since 1987, it has funded over 400 research projects in fields like mathematics, physics, biology, climate science, and computer science — producing thousands of joint research papers.

Research
🗣️

French language learning in India

French is the second most taught foreign language in Indian schools after English. The Alliance Française network across India teaches French to over 30,000 students every year. The DELF and DALF certificates — official French language qualifications — are taken by thousands of Indian students annually and are recognised worldwide.

Language
🎓

Indian students in France

Over 10,000 Indian students study in France every year — making India one of the top source countries for international students in France. France has actively worked to make itself more attractive by allowing post-study work visas, easing visa processing for Indian students, and offering many English-taught Masters programmes so language is no longer a barrier.

How to apply for the Charpak scholarship

If you're an Indian student interested in studying in France, here are the steps to apply.

1
Check eligibility

You must be an Indian citizen enrolled in an Indian university. Different tracks have different requirements — undergrad, Masters, or PhD level.

2
Register on Campus France India

Go to india.campusfrance.org and create an account. This is the official French government portal for Indian students applying to France.

3
Choose your programme and university

Browse French universities and programmes. Many offer English-taught Masters degrees so you don't need to know French to apply — though knowing French is a big advantage.

4
Submit your Charpak application

Applications typically open in January and close in March. You'll need your academic transcripts, a motivation letter, and two recommendation letters.

5
Visa and travel

Once selected, the French Embassy helps with your student visa. The Charpak scholarship covers your flight ticket, so you just need to pack your bags!

Did you know?

France abolished tuition fees for all international students at public universities in 2017 — and then reversed that decision in 2019 for non-EU students. However, Indian students on the Charpak scholarship are completely exempt from these fees. Even without a scholarship, French university fees are among the lowest in Europe — around €3,000–€4,000 per year for a Masters degree, compared to £20,000+ in the UK.

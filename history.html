Historical timeline

Key moments that shaped the relationship between India and France over three centuries.

1668

French East India Company arrives

France establishes its first trading post in Surat, beginning its commercial presence in India and competing with British and Dutch traders.

1674

Pondicherry founded

The French establish Pondicherry (Puducherry) as their main base in India. It becomes the capital of French India and remains French territory for nearly 300 years.

1741–1754

Dupleix and the battle for India

Governor-General Joseph François Dupleix nearly establishes French dominance over India, forming alliances with Indian rulers. Ultimately the British gain the upper hand.

1947

India gains independence

France is among the early nations to recognize independent India. Diplomatic relations are formally established, opening a new chapter beyond colonial history.

1954

Pondicherry transferred to India

After years of negotiation and peaceful protests, France transfers Pondicherry, Karaikal, Mahé, and Yanam to India. The French legacy in language and culture remains to this day.

1998

Strategic partnership signed

India and France sign a landmark Strategic Partnership agreement — one of the first such agreements India signed with any country. It covers defence, space, nuclear energy, and trade.

2023

Modi visits Paris — ties strengthened

PM Modi visits France as Guest of Honour at Bastille Day celebrations. Both nations announce major deals in defence, space, and green energy, marking a new high in bilateral relations.

Did you know?

Pondicherry still has French-style streets, French-named roads, and French architecture in the "White Town" area. Tamil and French are both official languages of Puducherry even today — making it one of the most unique places in India.

Les cinq piliers de l’avenir

The India-France relationship is evolving rapidly. These are the five areas where the partnership is expected to grow the most in the coming decade.

🌱

Climate & clean energy

Both India and France have committed to ambitious climate targets. The International Solar Alliance — co-founded by the two countries — is mobilising $1 trillion in solar investments. Joint projects in green hydrogen, offshore wind, and nuclear energy will be central to both nations meeting their net-zero goals. France brings nuclear expertise; India brings scale and manufacturing capacity.

Climate
🤖

Artificial intelligence & digital

The 2023 Digital Partnership between India and France created a framework for cooperation on AI, cybersecurity, and digital public infrastructure. India's massive digital ecosystem and France's AI research excellence are highly complementary. Joint AI research centres, startup partnerships, and data governance frameworks are all in development.

AI & digital
✈️

Defence & aerospace manufacturing

India's push for defence self-reliance under the "Make in India" programme creates huge opportunities for French companies. Dassault, Naval Group, and Safran are all looking to set up manufacturing in India. The new deal for 26 Rafale Marine jets includes significant technology transfer — meaning India will increasingly build and maintain advanced French military equipment on Indian soil.

Defence
🚀

Space exploration

ISRO and CNES are planning several new joint missions including TRISHNA for climate monitoring and continued collaboration on ocean observation satellites. As India ramps up its space programme — with lunar missions, a space station, and crewed spaceflight planned — France is positioned as a key partner providing sensors, instruments, and scientific expertise.

Space
🎓

Education & people-to-people ties

France has set a target of welcoming 20,000 Indian students per year by 2025. New joint degree programmes, expanded Charpak scholarships, and easier visa processes are all making France a top destination for Indian students. At the same time, French companies in India are creating demand for French-speaking Indian professionals — making French language skills increasingly valuable in the Indian job market.

Education
La vision indo-pacifique

France and India share a vision for a free, open, and rules-based Indo-Pacific — and are working together to make it happen.

Strategic alignment

🌊 A shared vision for the Indo-Pacific

France is the only European country with a permanent military presence in the Indian Ocean — through its overseas territories like Réunion and Mayotte. This gives France a unique stake in Indo-Pacific stability. India and France have aligned their Indo-Pacific strategies and are working together on maritime security, freedom of navigation, and counterbalancing Chinese influence in the region.

France has 1.6 million citizens and significant military assets in the Indian Ocean region — making it a natural partner for India's maritime security goals.

The two countries conduct regular naval patrols and exercises in the Indian Ocean through the VARUNA exercise series.

France strongly supports India's membership in the UN Security Council as a permanent member — a key Indian foreign policy goal.

Both nations are pushing for a multilateral Indo-Pacific framework that includes ASEAN, Australia, Japan, and other democracies — not dominated by any single power.

Points à surveille

Key milestones and projects coming up in the India-France relationship.

2025
TRISHNA satellite launch

The joint ISRO-CNES climate monitoring satellite is set to launch — measuring land surface temperatures to track urban heat islands and climate change.

2026
Jaitapur nuclear plant progress

The Jaitapur Nuclear Power Plant in Maharashtra — a joint India-France project — is expected to see significant construction milestones, moving toward becoming the world's largest nuclear plant.

2027
Rafale Marine delivery begins

The first of 26 Rafale Marine jets ordered for the Indian Navy are expected to be delivered — to be deployed on India's aircraft carrier INS Vikrant.

2030
20,000 Indian students in France

France's target of welcoming 20,000 Indian students per year — set during PM Modi's 2023 visit — is expected to be reached, making India the top source of international students in France.

2035+
Green hydrogen partnership

Joint India-France green hydrogen projects are expected to be fully operational — producing clean fuel for both domestic use and export, contributing to both nations' net-zero targets.

Saviez-vous?

At the 2023 Bastille Day celebrations in Paris, India was the Guest of Honour — and Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force contingents marched down the Champs-Élysées alongside French troops. An Indian Air Force Rafale flew in formation with French jets over Paris. It was one of the most visible symbols of how far the India-France relationship has come — and a glimpse of where it is headed.

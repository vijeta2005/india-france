ISRO & CNES — partners in space

India's ISRO and France's CNES (Centre National d'Études Spatiales) have one of the longest-running space cooperation agreements in the world — going back over 50 years.

Space cooperation

🛰️ 50+ years of exploring space together

ISRO and CNES signed their first cooperation agreement in 1964 — just three years after ISRO was founded. Since then they have jointly developed and launched multiple Earth observation satellites, collaborated on climate research missions, and shared critical space technology.

🛰️ Key joint satellite missions

2003
MEGHA-TROPIQUES

A joint climate research satellite that studied tropical rainfall and the water cycle. Provided crucial data on monsoons affecting both India and France's overseas territories.

2011
SARAL / AltiKa

An ocean observation satellite jointly developed by ISRO and CNES. It measures sea surface height and ocean currents with high precision.

2020
OCEANSAT-3 collaboration

France contributed instruments for India's ocean monitoring satellite, continuing decades of collaborative Earth observation work.

2025
TRISHNA (upcoming)

A new joint mission to monitor land surface temperature and urban heat islands — crucial for studying climate change impacts across Asia and Europe.

Other technology partnerships

Space is just the beginning — India and France collaborate across nuclear energy, digital technology, and green energy.

⚛️

Nuclear energy — Jaitapur

France's EDF (Électricité de France) is partnering with India's NPCIL to build the Jaitapur Nuclear Power Plant in Maharashtra. When completed, it will be the world's largest nuclear power plant by installed capacity — producing enough electricity for over 70 million homes. France is one of the world's leading nuclear energy nations and has been a key partner in India's civilian nuclear programme.

Nuclear energy
🌱

Green hydrogen & clean energy

India and France are jointly investing in green hydrogen technology as part of their climate commitments. French energy companies like TotalEnergies and Engie are partnering with Indian firms to build solar farms and green hydrogen production facilities. Both countries are founding members of the International Solar Alliance — an initiative launched jointly by India and France at the Paris Climate Summit in 2015.

Clean energy
💻

Digital & AI partnership

India and France signed a Digital Partnership in 2023 covering artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, digital public infrastructure, and data governance. Major French tech companies like Capgemini and Atos have large operations in India. Indian IT giants like TCS, Infosys, and Wipro have major delivery centres in France — creating deep digital ties between the two economies.

Digital & AI
🚄

Railways & smart cities

French company Alstom supplies metro trains and railway equipment to multiple Indian cities including Chennai, Kochi, and Lucknow. France has also partnered with India on smart city projects, contributing urban planning expertise and transport technology to rapidly growing Indian cities.

Infrastructure
Did you know?

The International Solar Alliance — co-founded by India and France at the Paris Climate Conference in 2015 — now has over 120 member countries. Its headquarters is in Gurugram, India, and its mission is to mobilise $1 trillion in solar energy investments by 2030. It is one of the most impactful global climate initiatives ever jointly launched by two countries.
